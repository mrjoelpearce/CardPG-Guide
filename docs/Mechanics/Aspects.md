Aspects are used during [Adventuring](../Mechanics/Adventuring.md).

These include your **Identity**, **Baggage**, **Drive**, and **Role**

They should be written as a double-edged sword, something that can sometimes help you and sometimes hinder you during adventure. For instance, if you are a [Goblin](../Ancestries/Goblin.md) [Swashbuckler](../Classes/Swashbuckler.md), you have the Identity:

*shaped by the underworld and the deals struck there*

When one of your aspects would be helpful in the situation, you can *invoke* one of your aspects by spending one of your [Fate Points](../Mechanics/Fate%20Points.md). You get +2 on your roll.

If our swashbuckler is trying to find a place to sell some illicit goods, he could spend a fate point to get a +2 to his *Underworld* roll to find the right part of town.

However, your aspects are not always beneficial. If you are trying to use *Personable* to make a good impression at court, you or the GM might suggest taking a *Compel*, rolling at -2 but gaining a Fate Point for your trouble.
