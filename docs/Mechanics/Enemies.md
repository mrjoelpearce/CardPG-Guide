## Minor Enemies
Most enemies you encounter will be minor enemies. Fights with normal enemies will not normally threaten a party, but they will cost you cards, so fights with normal enemies can leave you exhausted over time.

#### Normal Enemies
Normal enemies typically have between 5-10 HP. When you kill a minor enemy, each party member gets 1 gold.
#### Elite Enemies
Elite enemies will be marked with a gold star. Elite enemies drop 2 gold on death for each party member, and differ from regular enemies:
* +3 health
* +1 damage on all attacks
#### Champion Enemies
Champion enemies offer a more significant threat. They are marked with a gold crown. 
* +6 health
* +1 damage on all attacks
* Take 2 actions per round

## Major Enemies
The game also features major enemies, or bosses. Most dungeons will contain 2-3 bosses. Unlike minor enemies, bosses do not drop gold, but defeating a boss will usually trigger XP gain. As well, each boss will drop a *Trophy*, a piece of equipment that can only be used once. Trophies are powerful enough to shift the tide of a fight.

#### Regular Bosses
Regular bosses are designed to present a genuine threat to players, especially if they are supported by some minor enemies. Many bosses will have multiple phases, with new moves for each phase. Like players, bosses select 2 actions per turn, and will always act on the faster of the two actions.

#### Bounty Bosses
Most dungeons will also feature at least one *Bounty Boss*, which are normally not part of the main story of the dungeon. These bosses will be marked with a red skull. These are the most powerful enemies in the game, and will be a challenge for even a fully rested party. Bounty Bosses will not attack players on sight, so it will always be up to the party whether to engage with these enemies. Bounty Bosses often guard troves of treasure.
