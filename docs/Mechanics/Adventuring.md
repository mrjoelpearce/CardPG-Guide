As you travel to and explore dungeons, you will face a number of challenges, ranging from traps to social encounters to traversal puzzles and hidden pathways.

When you attempt a challenging action, the GM will give you a difficulty, ranging from trivial (0) to impossible (7). You will roll 4DF and add the relevant skill.

**Roll Results**

* Below the target: failure
* Meet the target: Succeed with drawback
* Exceed the target: Succeed
* Succeed with at least 3 over target: Succeed with benefit

You will also have a pool of **[Fate Points](../Mechanics/Fate%20Points.md)**. you begin each session with 3 fate points. Players earn fate points by:

* Offering a compel (using an aspect as a disadvantage)
* Accepting a compel (accepting when the GM suggests using an aspect as a disadvantage)
* Recovering a point through the combat Luck Die.

Players can spend Fate Points to:

* Refuse a compel from the GM
* Reroll the dice
* Invoke an aspect (use an aspect to get +2 on a roll)
